Two Eras of Thought

The fields underwent a dramatic shift, moving from a linear view of progress to a critical analysis of power and inequality.

The Foundational View (pre-1990s)

Early work focused on how Western biomedicine spread to other societies, viewing it as a "carrier of modernity."

  • →Primary Question: How do "traditional" societies adopt "modern" medicine?
  • →Limitation: Often overlooked power dynamics and assumed a one-size-fits-all path to progress.

The Modern Turn (post-1990s)

A critical shift rejected this linear model, instead centering on power, politics, and historical context.

  • →Key Concept: Focus on "Structural Violence" & "Neoliberalism" to see how systems cause illness.
  • →Goal: To critique, analyze, and participate in solving complex global health issues.
